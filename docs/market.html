Why nobody has shipped this

The intersection is too narrow.

Building this requires four overlapping skills that almost never live in one team.

1

Salesforce platform fluency

LWC, LWS, static resources, Reports API, packaging — most JS devs don't have it.

2

PDF engine fluency

jsPDF quirks, fonts, image scaling, page break math — most Salesforce devs don't have it.

3

Reports API fluency

Fact maps, grouping, governor limits — even most senior Apex devs avoid this corner.

4

Discipline to say no

Every previous attempt defaulted to Visualforce or external services. We resisted.

Honest risks

What could go wrong — and what we'd do about it.

Salesforce ships native PDF export

Mitigation: Salesforce has had 12 years to ship this and hasn't. Even if they did, they wouldn't ship multi-dashboard briefing books or white-labeled branding — that's where we'd own the category.

Conga / S-Docs add dashboard support

Mitigation: They'd have to rewrite their server-side engines as client-side LWCs — a multi-year effort. We'll have 12+ months of head start and a defensive moat in the technical pipeline.

Slow AppExchange security review

Mitigation: Pure client-side rendering means minimal review surface — no callouts, no DML on standard objects, no shared state. Other native LWC packages have passed in 4–6 weeks.

Two-person bus factor

Mitigation: Document everything publicly. Open-source the engine (free tier). Build community around the project from day one.
